How To Read This Index

Human metabolites often exist as mixtures of protonation, salt, stereochemical, and enzyme-bound states. The formulas below use common neutral or dominant physiological shorthand. Complex cofactors and polymers are shown with biochemical structural shorthand, such as CH3-C(=O)-S-CoA, because expanding every atom in CoA, NAD, glycogen, or a protein would make the page less useful.
Molecular formula Element counts for the molecule or its common biochemical ion. Charges are shown when central to the reaction.
Condensed structure Readable linear structural formula, ring shorthand, or cofactor shorthand.
Representative reaction A reaction from the atlas showing how the compound is made, consumed, or interconverted.
